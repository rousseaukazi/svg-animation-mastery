01 — feTurbulence

Liquid
Morphing

Organic blob backgrounds driven by SVG displacement filters. Every frame is unique. Zero canvas, zero images.

How it works

Four SVG <circle> elements are wrapped in a feDisplacementMap filter that reads noise from an feTurbulence primitive. As the turbulence baseFrequency is incremented every frame via requestAnimationFrame, the displacement field shifts organically, warping the circles into unpredictable blob shapes.

GSAP then independently floats each blob (translate + scale) on different durations, creating the layered, breathing effect. The three-color gradient inside each circle adds depth without any extra DOM elements.

1

feTurbulence generates noise — fractalNoise at low baseFrequency creates smooth, cloud-like patterns that change slowly over time.

2

feDisplacementMap warps circles — xChannelSelector="R" yChannelSelector="G" uses the red and green noise channels to displace pixels horizontally and vertically.

3

GSAP floats the blobs — each circle tweens x/y position and scale on a different duration with yoyo: true, repeat: -1 for infinite, non-looping motion.

4

rAF updates baseFrequency — a smooth sinusoidal easing applied in JS prevents jarring frequency jumps, keeping motion organic.

Particle Constellation

Particles drift across a canvas. Proximity lines form dynamically. Your cursor repels them. Pure canvas, pure performance.

Canvas 2D requestAnimationFrame proximity detection mouse repulsion

How it works

Every frame, each particle's velocity is updated based on proximity to the cursor: particles within 100px are pushed away proportionally to their closeness. Velocities are capped and damped by 0.99 per frame so they drift to rest naturally after being disturbed.

Proximity lines are drawn between any two particles within 140px of each other. The line opacity is proportional to closeness — (1 - dist / maxDist) * 0.5 — creating a dynamic constellation effect without any pre-defined connections.

On mobile (window.innerWidth < 768) the particle count is reduced to 30 to maintain smooth 60fps on lower-powered devices. An IntersectionObserver pauses the rAF loop when the canvas is off-screen, preserving battery.

1

Particle initialization — Each particle gets a random position, velocity (±0.2), radius (1–3px), and an HSL color in the 240–320° range for the purple-blue palette.

2

Mouse repulsion physics — force = (repelDist - dist) / repelDist * repelForce gives a linear falloff: strongest at the cursor, zero at the boundary.

3

O(n²) proximity check optimized — Only the upper triangle of the particle pair matrix is checked (j > i). For 70 particles this is ~2,415 checks per frame — fast enough for 60fps.

4

IntersectionObserver for performance — The rAF loop starts and stops as the canvas enters/exits the viewport. On mobile, particle count is halved to maintain frame rate.
